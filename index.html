Overview

Abstract

We present a first unified framework that jointly learns relightable 3D Gaussians and a neural Signed Distance Field (SDF) through coarse-to-fine bidirectional 3D-to-3D supervision, enabling accurate geometry reconstruction and Physically-Based Rendering (PBR) with storage efficiency.

While recent 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) works extend beyond Novel-View Synthesis (NVS) to mesh reconstruction and PBR, existing methods infer 3D properties indirectly from rendered 2D images, producing coarse meshes and unreliable BRDF-lighting decomposition. To address these limitations, our approach jointly optimizes SDF and 3DGS directly in 3D space.

Depth‑Guided SDF Alignment uses Gaussian depth maps to anchor SDF rays and refine SDF normals via pixel‑wise depth gradients. Normal‑Guided 3DGS Alignment projects Gaussians onto the SDF surface, supervises Gaussian normals with SDF normals, and applies normal‑aware densification to capture fine‑scale geometry.

To mitigate excessive Gaussian splitting during normal‑aware densification, we propose Dual Density Control, which effectively balances geometric fidelity and memory efficiency. The precisely aligned geometry supplies accurate normals for differentiable PBR, enabling faithful Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function and lighting decomposition.

Experiments on standard benchmarks demonstrate State-Of-The-Art performance in NVS, mesh reconstruction, and PBR while using the fewer Gaussian primitives.

Comparison

Quantitative Comparison

Our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both object-level and scene-level novel view synthesis benchmarks, demonstrating superior PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS metrics compared to existing approaches.
